Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu, a timeless Italian dessert, is an exquisite symphony of flavors and textures that captivates the senses. Its name, "tiramisu," translates to "pick-me-up" or "lift-me-up," and that's precisely what this desert does. Each indulgent bite is a harmonious blend of delicate coffee-soaked ladyfingers, layered with a velvety mascarpone cheese mixture. The coffee infusion offers a gentle bitterness, beautifully balanced by the sweetness of the creamy mascarpone

Ingredients

  • Strong Coffee
  • Coffee Liqueur
  • Mascarpone Cheese
  • Egg Yolks
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Vanilla Extract
  • Ladyfingers
  • Cocoa Powder
  • Dark Chocolate
  • Whipped Cream
  • Chocolate Shavings

Steps

  1. Brew strong coffee and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. In a mixing bowl, beat egg yolks and sugar together until pale and creamy.
  3. Add mascarpone cheese and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth and well combined.
  4. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture, ensuring they are soaked but not soggy.
  5. Arrange a layer of soaked ladyfingers in the bottom of a serving dish (e.g., a trifle dish or a rectangular dish).
  6. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
  7. Create another layer of soaked ladyfingers on top of the mascarpone mixture.
  8. Add the remaining mascarpone mixture on top.
  9. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. Chilling allows the flavors to meld and the dessert to set.
  10. Before serving, dust the top of the Tiramisu with cocoa powder using a fine sieve.
  11. Optionally, garnish with freshly whipped cream and chocolate shavings or curls.
